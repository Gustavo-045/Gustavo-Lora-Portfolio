Atomic Devotion

Un oscuro misterio acecha en una planta nuclear abandonada durante la Guerra Fría. Debes descubrir la verdad detrás de la catástrofe y sobrevivir a los horrores ocultos en la instalación.

Mi Enfoque

As designer and programmer for Atomic Devotion, I developed a first-person survival horror game with strong influences from Silent Hill and Resident Evil, set in a nuclear power plant in Kansas during the Cold War. I was in charge of structuring the game's progression, designing an experience that combines exploration, puzzle solving, and tense confrontations with mutated creatures. I designed an immersive narrative, integrating psychological and environmental elements to generate a constant feeling of oppression and danger. In terms of programming, I implemented the game's core mechanics in Unity, ensuring fluid control and precise interaction with the environment. I developed inventory systems, doors, and interactive objects, as well as enemy AI, which responds dynamically to the player. I also optimized lighting and visual effects to recreate a PSX-style low-poly aesthetic, enhancing the game's atmosphere without sacrificing performance. In terms of level design, I organized the structure of the nuclear power plant, dividing it into different floors and establishing a clear progression through objectives and puzzles. I made sure that each area offered unique challenges, while maintaining a balance between exploration and tension. The arrangement of enemies, resources, and key points was planned to reinforce the narrative and foster player immersion. This project allowed me to develop a comprehensive view of horror game design and programming, combining storytelling, game mechanics, and technical optimization. My approach was focused on creating a complete and cohesive experience, where each element contributes to the atmosphere and gameplay. Atomic Devotion is an example of my ability to conceptualize, design, and implement a video game with a unique identity and well-structured gameplay.
